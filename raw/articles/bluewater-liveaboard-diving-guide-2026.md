# Liveaboard Diving - How to Plan, What to Expect & Insider Tips for 2026

Source page: [Bluewater Dive Travel - Liveaboard Diving Guide 2026](https://www.bluewaterdivetravel.com/article/-liveaboard-diving-how-to-plan-what-to-expect-insider-tips-for-2026)

## Overview

Bluewater Dive Travel explains why liveaboard diving remains one of the most popular ways to explore major dive destinations in 2026. The article is a general planning guide, but it explicitly names the Maldives as one of the classic liveaboard destinations.

## What a Liveaboard Is

A liveaboard is a dive vessel where guests eat, sleep, and dive from the same platform. Trips typically last 7 to 14 nights and can include four or five dives per day.

Liveaboards are especially useful in places with remote sites, and the article names the Maldives among those destinations.

## How to Choose a Liveaboard

The guide says the right boat depends on:

- Experience level.
- Comfort and amenities.
- Dive focus.

It notes that some itineraries are suited to strong currents, deeper dives, and advanced conditions, while others are built around comfort, big animals, macro photography, or underwater workshops.

## Preparing for a Trip

The article recommends:

- Advanced Open Water and Nitrox certification.
- Dive gear and spares.
- Reef-safe sunscreen.
- Motion sickness medication.
- Soft-sided luggage.
- Dive-specific insurance with medical evacuation coverage.

## Safety

Bluewater says reputable liveaboards maintain emergency oxygen, first aid equipment, and detailed dive planning. Divers are expected to respect depth limits, buddy procedures, and surface intervals.

## Why the Maldives Fits the Model

The article highlights the Maldives as a perennial favorite for:

- Channel dives.
- Manta cleaning stations.
- Whale shark encounters.

## First-Time Liveaboard Tips

The guide's practical advice includes:

- Arrive rested and hydrated.
- Label dive gear.
- Bring spare O-rings and batteries.
- Pace yourself and skip a dive if needed rather than burning out.
