# 37 Stunning Hotels and Resorts in the Maldives

Source page: [Condé Nast Traveler - The Best Hotels in the Maldives](https://www.cntraveler.com/gallery/the-best-hotels-in-the-maldives)

## Overview

Condé Nast Traveler frames the Maldives as a benchmark destination for private-island luxury. The guide notes that overwater stays are standard, privacy is expected, and many properties now pair luxury with conservation, coral programs, wellness, and solar power.

## How the List Is Curated

The editors say each hotel was selected independently by writers who know the destination and have stayed at the property. They look for:

- Beautiful design.
- Great location.
- Warm service.
- Sustainability credentials.
- Authentically interesting or lesser-known properties as well as classic luxury names.

The list is updated regularly as hotels open and evolve.

## Resort Themes Highlighted

The article highlights a few clear Maldives patterns:

- Overwater villas are common.
- Spas often sit over lagoons.
- Restaurants may be underwater or dramatically positioned.
- Conservation and reef restoration are increasingly central.

## Luxury and Eco Notes

Several properties are singled out for sustainability and conservation:

- Four Seasons properties are credited with resident marine biologists and reef-restoration programs.
- Six Senses Laamu is noted for seagrass protection.
- Soneva is praised for waste reduction and recycling efforts.
- Kudadoo is described as fully solar powered.
- JOALI BEING, Patina Maldives, and The Ritz-Carlton Maldives also appear in the responsible-hospitality context on the page.

## Best For

The article specifically recommends:

- Couples: Cheval Blanc Randheli and Four Seasons Landaa Giraavaru.
- Families: JW Marriott Maldives Resort and Spa and Fairmont Maldives, Sirru Fen Fen.
- All-inclusive stays: Kudadoo.
- Underwater dining: Hurawalhi.
- Wellness: COMO Cocoa Island.
- Private island escapes: Coco Prive Kuda Hithi.
