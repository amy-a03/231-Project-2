# Maldives Guesthouses : The Rise of Local Islands

Source page: [Dreaming of Maldives - Maldives Guesthouses](https://www.dreamingofmaldives.com/maldives-guesthouses/)

## Overview

Dreaming of Maldives explains how guesthouses transformed Maldivian tourism. The article traces the shift from a resort-only model to local-island accommodation and shows how guesthouses opened the country to travelers on smaller budgets.

## Why Guesthouses Matter

The page says guesthouses:

- Lower the cost of staying in the Maldives.
- Let travelers experience real island communities.
- Support local tourism income.
- Make the destination more accessible beyond luxury resorts.

## What a Guesthouse Stay Feels Like

The article says a guesthouse stay is very different from a resort stay. Travelers are in a working local community, where fishing boats leave early, children go to school, and cafés open through the day.

## Activities Offered

The page says guesthouses often organize:

- Snorkeling trips.
- Diving excursions.
- Traditional fishing.
- Sandbank visits.
- Island hopping.

Some also offer small wellness services.

## Practical Differences on Local Islands

The article emphasizes:

- Alcohol is forbidden on local islands.
- Modest dress is required.
- Beaches are public rather than resort-managed.
- Bikini beaches are now common in guesthouse destinations.
- Some excursions are boat-based rather than beach-accessed.

## Budget Reality

The page says guesthouses made the Maldives accessible to a broader range of travelers, but transportation and excursions can still make the destination expensive if not planned carefully.

## Notable Island Mentions

The article specifically references:

- Ukulhas, for a strong house reef and snorkeling.
- Dhigurah, for whale shark encounters and beautiful beaches.
- Maafushi, for its designated bikini beach.
- Utheemu, as an example of a quieter local-island experience.
