# A tale of two islands: How Four Seasons Resorts Maldives are setting the standard for sustainable luxury

Source page: [EarthCheck case study](https://earthcheck.org/case-studies/four-seasons-maldives-sustainable-luxury/)

## Overview

EarthCheck profiles Four Seasons Resorts Maldives at Landaa Giraavaru and Kuda Huraa as a case study in sustainable luxury. The article frames the two properties as examples of how high-end resort operations can coexist with environmental stewardship in a fragile island ecosystem.

## Key Themes

The case study focuses on:

- Third-party sustainability benchmarking.
- Coral restoration.
- Turtle rescue and rehabilitation.
- Local training and employment.
- Lower-impact operations.
- Marine education and citizen science.

## Quantified Impact

The page highlights several large-scale metrics:

- More than 500,000 coral fragments planted since 2001.
- More than 280 sea turtles rescued, rehabilitated, and released.
- More than 850 young Maldivians trained through the Four Seasons Apprenticeship Program.

## Operational Sustainability

EarthCheck describes the resorts as embedding sustainability into day-to-day operations through:

- Phasing out single-use plastics in food and beverage.
- Recycled or sustainably sourced dive-shop items.
- Local and ethical procurement.
- Ongoing staff training and benchmarking.

The resorts also track items such as coral propagation, reef-safe sunscreen promotion, tree planting, and sustainable seafood sourcing.

## Marine Conservation

The article explains that the resorts launched one of the Maldives' earliest large-scale coral restoration programs after the 1998 bleaching event. It also notes the Marine Discovery Centres as hubs for:

- Coral frame building.
- Turtle releases.
- Marine education.
- Citizen science.

## Community and Culture

The case study says Four Seasons works with local councils, schools, universities, and nearby communities, while also supporting cultural programming such as Boduberu drumming and lacquerware demonstrations.

## Certification Context

EarthCheck says the resorts have EarthCheck Silver certification and are working toward Gold certification.
