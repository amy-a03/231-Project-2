# Best Time to Visit Maldives by Month

Source page: [Enchanting Travels - Best Time To Visit Maldives](https://www.enchantingtravels.com/destinations/asia/maldives/best-time-to-visit-maldives/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

## Overview

Enchanting Travels describes the Maldives as a year-round tropical beach destination with moderate equatorial weather. The ideal travel period is the high season from December through April, when the dry monsoon brings lower rainfall and humidity.

## Month-by-Month Notes

### January

January brings blue skies, warm days, and excellent diving conditions. It is one of the best and most popular months to visit. Short afternoon or evening rain spells are still possible.

Recommended activities:

- Dive at Banana Reef.
- Try paddle boarding.

### February

February is the driest month of the year. Humidity is low, and water conditions are ideal for snorkeling and other underwater activities. Average temperature is around 82°F.

Recommended activity:

- Swim with whale sharks.

### March

March is described as the hottest month, with an average temperature around 84°F. Humidity is slightly higher than in February, and it remains high season, so advance booking is recommended.

Recommended activities:

- Use the resort's house reef for easy snorkeling or diving.
- Try parasailing for aerial island views.

### April

April marks the end of high season. Rain risk increases, especially toward the end of the month, but showers are usually brief and followed by sunshine. April can be good for travelers seeking deals.

Recommended activities:

- Take a seaplane flight.
- Plan a romantic beachside dinner.

### May

May brings more rainfall, higher humidity, and more unpredictable weather, including possible storms. Water visibility is lower, so it is not ideal for diving. The page also notes May as the start of Ramadan.

Recommended activities:

- Relax in a hammock with ocean views.
- Use the resort infinity pool.

### June

The southwest monsoon is established by June, bringing cloudy days, frequent rain, and possible heavy downpours. Enchanting Travels recommends southern atolls over northern atolls for travelers seeking lower resort prices because southern atolls may see fewer rainy days.

Recommended activities:

- Visit local villages.
- See bioluminescent plankton on Vaadhoo Island.

### July

Rainy days decline in the northern atolls from around 18 to 15-16, while they increase slightly in the southern atolls. July is a good month for surfing, with average swells around 2 to 8 feet.

Recommended activities:

- Experience Independence Day celebrations.
- Surf larger waves.

### August

August is hot and humid, with heavy but short downpours. Plankton lowers water visibility. Resorts offer discounts, making it a popular month despite the wet-season conditions.

Recommended activities:

- Swim with manta rays and whale sharks.
- Visit the Male fish market.

### September

September has frequent heavy rain and temperatures in the mid-80s. It is still a good time to spot manta rays and sharks, although visibility is reduced.

Recommended activities:

- Stay in an overwater villa.
- Practice seaside yoga.

### October

October is the wettest month, though the southwest monsoon begins to ease by the end of the month. Water remains warm, and it is the last good month for surfing.

Recommended activities:

- Book a spa treatment.
- Tour Mulee'aage Palace and the National Museum in Male.

### November

Tourists return in November as rain gives way to brighter days, with occasional afternoon showers. Northern atolls are recommended over southern atolls for beach stays because they have fewer rainy days.

Recommended activities:

- Take a sunset cruise and watch for dolphins.
- Snorkel around Naifaru Island.

### December

December is high season, and resorts are often booked months ahead. The second half of the month is especially warm and welcoming for beach escapes. Sea temperature is around 82°F, supporting underwater activities and water sports.

Recommended activities:

- Celebrate New Year's Eve.
- Sail and try big-game fishing.

## Climate

The Maldives has two monsoon patterns:

- Northeast monsoon: dry season.
- Southwest monsoon: wet season.

Because of the Maldives' equatorial location, major cyclones are not typical. The drier months run from November through April, with March and April described as the hottest months. Monsoon season runs from May to early November. June, July, and August often bring the heaviest rainfall, especially in the southern atolls.

## Packing Guidance

### Peak Season: October-November and February-May

Temperature range listed: 73°F to 88°F. Expect warm weather, sunshine, some humidity, and occasional showers. Pack beachwear, sunglasses, sunscreen, breathable light clothes, and flip-flops. Carry a scarf or light jacket for visits to Male because respectful dress is required in town.

### Winter: December-January

Temperature range listed: 73.4°F to 87.8°F. Expect dry weather, calm seas, and higher resort humidity. Pack light clothes, beachwear, and a rain jacket.

### Monsoon: June-August

Temperature range listed: 80.6°F to 91.4°F. Expect cloudier skies, rougher seas, and higher humidity. Pack beachwear, a rain jacket or umbrella, and flip-flops that dry easily.
