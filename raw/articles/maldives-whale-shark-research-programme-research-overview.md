# Research Overview

Source page: [Maldives Whale Shark Research - Research Overview](https://maldiveswhalesharkresearch.org/research/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

## Overview

The research overview explains that the programme's core aim is to understand whale shark population dynamics in the Maldives and how the species fits into the wider global picture.

## Key Findings Highlighted

The page notes research into:

- Movements.
- Size.
- Sex ratios.
- Threats.
- Environmental influences such as lunar cycles and tides.

## Big Fish Network Stats

The page gives a snapshot that includes:

- 185 contributors.
- 726 whale sharks.
- More than 8,000 encounters.
- A 72.5% male sex bias.

## South Ari Marine Protected Area

The overview says MWSR's work helped provide baseline data for the creation of the Maldives' largest protected area, the South Ari Marine Protected Area.

## Conservation Relevance

The programme emphasizes that open access and free distribution of information help visitors and local operators benefit from whale sharks while supporting long-term conservation.
