# Reef manta rays forage on tidally driven, high density patches in Hanifaru Bay, Maldives

Source page: [Manta Trust Research Portal - Armstrong et al. 2021](https://research.mantatrust.org/armstrong-et-al-2021)

## Overview

This research examines how reef manta rays feed in Hanifaru Bay, Maldives, by linking manta behavior with zooplankton density and environmental conditions.

## Main Finding

The study found that manta feeding events were strongly associated with high zooplankton biomass and that manta rays switched from non-feeding to feeding behavior at a prey density threshold of 53.7 mg dry mass per cubic meter.

## Why Hanifaru Bay Matters

The abstract says Hanifaru Bay is a key feeding hotspot with tidally driven zooplankton patches, making it an important management area for reef manta rays.

## Conservation Value

The study helps explain the conditions that lead to large manta aggregations and supports management of this economically important marine protected area.
