# Learning about zooplankton from the experts - the hungry manta rays of Hanifaru Bay!

Source page: [Manta Trust - Learning about zooplankton](https://www.mantatrust.org/the-cyclone-exclusive-content/2022/01/27/learning-about-zooplankton)

## Overview

Manta Trust describes Hanifaru Bay in the Maldives as the largest known aggregation site for feeding reef manta rays, with more than 250 individuals identified during peak feeding events.

## What the Story Explains

The article explains that during the southwest monsoon, manta rays feed on nutrient-rich zooplankton that is trapped and concentrated by bay geometry, currents, tides, and plankton movements.

## Field Research

The team used freedivers to tow plankton nets near feeding mantas because standard towing was not possible in the protected bay. They then analyzed the samples to better understand prey, predators, and the surrounding environment.

## Why It Matters

The story says warming oceans may reduce tropical zooplankton biomass, which would directly affect manta rays and whale sharks that depend on these prey pulses.
