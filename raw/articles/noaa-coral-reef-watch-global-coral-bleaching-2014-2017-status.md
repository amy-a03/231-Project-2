# Global Coral Bleaching 2014-2017: Status and an Appeal for Observations

Source page: [NOAA Coral Reef Watch - Global Coral Bleaching 2014-2017 Status](https://coralreefwatch.noaa.gov/satellite/analyses_guidance/global_coral_bleaching_2014-17_status.php)

## Overview

NOAA Coral Reef Watch reviews the 2014-2017 global coral bleaching event as the third documented global bleaching event and describes it as the longest, most widespread, and possibly the most damaging on record.

## What the Page Explains

The page ties the event to:

- A strong El Niño in 2015-2016.
- Prolonged heat stress across many reef regions.
- Severe bleaching and mortality in multiple ocean basins.

The report explains that heat stress can be tracked through NOAA's daily 5km satellite bleaching alert area and its four-month heat-stress outlook.

## Global Severity

The page says:

- More than 70% of the world's coral reefs experienced bleaching-level heat stress during the event.
- Alert Level 2 heat stress corresponds to widespread bleaching and significant mortality.
- Alert Level 1 indicates significant bleaching.

## Ocean-Basin Impacts

The report highlights impacts across:

- The Pacific.
- The Atlantic.
- The Indian Ocean.
- Southeast Asia.
- Australia.
- The Caribbean.

## Maldives Relevance

The broader NOAA Coral Reef Watch product suite and related documentation show that the Maldives has been part of coral-bleaching monitoring and that the Indian Ocean was affected by bleaching stress during this era.
