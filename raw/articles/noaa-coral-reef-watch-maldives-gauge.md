# Maldives

Source page: [NOAA Coral Reef Watch Maldives Gauge](https://coralreefwatch.noaa.gov/product/vs/gauges/maldives.php)

## Overview

NOAA Coral Reef Watch's Maldives regional virtual station is part of its 5km near-real-time bleaching monitoring network. The Maldives entry is used to track current thermal stress relevant to coral bleaching risk.

## Current Status Captured

In the accessible regional station list, Maldives appears with:

- Current stress level: Bleaching Warning
- Change from last update: Alert: (+) Bleaching Warning

## How to Read the Product

The regional virtual station system is designed to combine 5km satellite data across a jurisdiction and surface:

- Current stress levels.
- Time-series graphs.
- Data files.
- Email alerts.

The product note also warns that some station pages still use the older alert-level system until the update to the expanded bleaching alert levels is complete.

## Why It Matters for Maldives

The Maldives entry indicates active reef heat stress monitoring for the country and gives a live signal that reef managers and visitors can use to understand bleaching pressure.

This file captures the Maldives gauge in summarized form. Consult the original Coral Reef Watch station list for the latest stress level, graphs, and downloaded data files.
