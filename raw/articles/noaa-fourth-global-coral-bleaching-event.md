# NOAA Confirms Fourth Global Coral Bleaching Event

Source page: [NOAA - NOAA Confirms Fourth Global Coral Bleaching Event](https://www.noaa.gov/news-release/noaa-confirms-4th-global-coral-bleaching-event)

## Overview

NOAA says the world is currently experiencing the fourth global coral bleaching event on record, and the second in the last 10 years. The release ties the event to extensive bleaching-level heat stress across the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Ocean basins.

## What NOAA Says

The release states that Coral Reef Watch's satellite-based heat-stress monitoring has tracked bleaching-level conditions across the world using sea-surface-temperature data that extends from 1985 to the present.

## Why It Matters

The article frames global bleaching as a large-scale, current environmental event rather than a historical one. It also points readers to Coral Reef Watch as the operational monitoring system behind the alerting.

## Maldives Relevance

Because the Indian Ocean is included in the affected basins, this release is directly relevant to Maldives reef conditions and tourism-linked marine ecosystems.
