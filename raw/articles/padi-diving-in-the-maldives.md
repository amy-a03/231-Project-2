# Diving in the Maldives

Source page: [PADI - Diving in the Maldives](https://www.padi.com/diving-in/maldives/)

## Overview

PADI describes the Maldives as a low-lying nation with white sand beaches and excellent reefs packed with marine life. The diving is often done at a relaxed drift pace because of the monsoon current that sweeps along the island chains, carrying nutrients and divers along the walls and channels.

The page emphasizes atoll diving, thilas, channels, caverns, overhangs, and cleaning stations. It also highlights manta rays, giant turtles, and whale sharks as signature marine-life encounters.

## When to Dive

The Maldives can be dived year-round because water temperatures stay warm, roughly 80°F to 86°F. PADI says:

- August through November is the best period for manta rays and whale sharks.
- January through April offers the best visibility and the driest, warmest weather.

## Marine Life and Underwater Topography

PADI highlights:

- Drift diving along current-swept walls.
- Thilas rising from lagoon floors.
- Swim-throughs, caverns, and overhangs.
- Colorful sponges, invertebrates, and gorgonians.
- Cleaning stations where manta rays are serviced by wrasse and shrimp.
- Common sightings of turtles and whale sharks.

## Getting There

The main international gateway is Velana International Airport in North Malé Atoll, near Malé. The page also notes other airports with more limited international connections, including Hanimaadhoo and Gan.

## Planning Notes

- Maldives is a strong liveaboard destination.
- Dive currents can be significant, so the destination suits relaxed drift diving and experienced planning.
- The page presents PADI dive guides, dive resorts, and liveaboards as ways to plan a trip.
