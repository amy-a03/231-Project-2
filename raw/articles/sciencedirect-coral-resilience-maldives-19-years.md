# Coral resilience in a changing climate: A site-specific analysis of Maldivian reefs over 19 years

Source page: [ScienceDirect abstract](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352485525004086)

## Overview

This 2025 paper analyzes 19 years of data from four Maldivian reefs to examine how site characteristics and human pressure influence coral resilience.

## Main Findings

The abstract says:

- Reefs with minimal human impact recovered better.
- Lagoon sites and heavily visited reefs showed more variable recovery.
- Rasdhoo Madivaru, an oceanic site, maintained stable coral cover.
- Disturbance and recovery differed across sites and time periods.

## Methods Mentioned

The study used Reef Check surveys to assess:

- Benthic composition.
- Fish abundance.
- Macro-invertebrate abundance.

## Why It Matters

The paper reinforces the idea that reef response in the Maldives is highly site-specific and shaped by both warming and local human use.
