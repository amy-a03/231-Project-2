# Scuba Diving Guide to Maldives

Source page: [Scuba Diving - Guide to Maldives](https://www.scubadiving.com/travel/dive-guide/maldives)

## Overview

Scuba Diving presents the Maldives as a bucket-list destination with crystal-clear waters, warm temperatures, and easy sightings of manta rays, whale sharks, reef sharks, sea turtles, and eagle rays. The article is aimed at divers planning a trip and emphasizes both marine life and practical travel logistics.

## Why Divers Go

The guide says the Maldives has:

- Warm water in the low to mid 80s year-round.
- Strong marine-life encounters.
- A reputation for clear water and memorable reefs.
- Options ranging from guesthouses to high-end resorts and liveaboards.

## Best Time for Marine Life

The article recommends May through November for the biggest wildlife encounters, especially in Baa, Ari, and North Mal areas. These regions are known for whale sharks and manta rays, which are drawn by plankton blooms.

It also notes that South Ari Marine Park is a strong year-round area for whale sharks and manta rays.

## Liveaboards

The guide explains that liveaboards are a strong option for divers who want:

- Lodging, food, and dives arranged together.
- Flexibility to follow fish and current conditions.
- Access to multiple sites without staying anchored to one resort.

It notes that advanced certification is often required for intense itineraries.

## Resort and Transfer Notes

The Maldives has over 180 resorts, but some require seaplane transfers that only fly in daylight and can cost several hundred dollars round-trip. The article notes that arrangements are usually handled by the resort.

## Practical Basics

The article also includes:

- Dhivehi as the local language, with English widely used in tourism.
- Maldivian rufiyaa as currency.
- Velana International Airport as the main airport.
- Alcohol restrictions, with sales limited to private resorts and liveaboards.
