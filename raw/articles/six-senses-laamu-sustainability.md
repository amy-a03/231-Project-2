# Maldives Ecotourism & Responsible Travel | Six Senses Laamu

Source page: [Six Senses Laamu Sustainability](https://www.sixsenses.com/en/hotels-resorts/asia-the-pacific/maldives/laamu/sustainability/)

## Overview

Six Senses Laamu presents sustainability as central to the resort's identity. The property is located in the only atoll-designated Hope Spot in the Maldives and is surrounded by Marine Protected Areas. The page blends environmental protection, marine science, and social responsibility.

## Environmental Responsibility

The resort highlights the Maldives Underwater Initiative, a collaboration with the Manta Trust, Blue Marine Foundation, and Olive Ridley Project. The program supports:

- Marine research.
- Guest education.
- Community engagement.
- Reef and seagrass protection.

## Key Marine Metrics

The page lists 2024 achievements including:

- 1,350 hours of scientific surveys.
- 14,061 megafauna sightings.
- 1,268 sea turtles identified.
- 1,839 community members engaged.
- 13,221 kilograms of sustainable local seafood sourced.
- 335,024 square meters of seagrass meadows protected.
- 2,862,000 coral larvae released.
- 703 manta ray sightings.
- 1,597 turtle hatchlings reaching the ocean.

## SHELL and Earth Lab

The Sea Hub of Environmental Learning in Laamu, or SHELL, is the resort's marine science research and education center. The page also refers to an Earth Lab for self-sufficiency and sustainability education.

## Coral and Seagrass Work

The resort says it can grow 10,000 baby corals and has released nearly 3 million larvae onto its house reef. It also works to protect seagrass meadows, which it describes as important habitat and a conservation priority.

## Social Responsibility

Six Senses says it works with local fishers through a sustainable seafood program and collaborates with community partners to support livelihoods and reduce ecological impact.
