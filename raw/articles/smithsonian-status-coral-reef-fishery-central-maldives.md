# Status of the coral reef fishery in an atoll country under tourism development: The case of Central Maldives

Source document: [Smithsonian repository PDF](https://repository.si.edu/server/api/core/bitstreams/137427a1-2141-4108-8b20-72ec9d42e667/content)

## Overview

This paper examines reef fishery conditions in Central Maldives and how tourism development changed demand for local fish.

## Main Findings

The abstract says:

- Tourism increased demand for fresh fish that resorts buy directly from local fishermen.
- Catch composition in the 2006-2007 survey did not show an alarming trend.
- Yields remained below published unsustainable-fishery thresholds.
- The authors still warn that changes should be monitored closely as tourism demand grows.

## Why It Matters

The study is a useful baseline for reef-fish management because it ties fishery status to tourism growth and to the need for continued monitoring.
