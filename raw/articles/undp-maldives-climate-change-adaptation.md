# Maldives

Source page: [UNDP Climate Change Adaptation - Maldives](https://www.adaptation-undp.org/explore/asia-and-pacific/maldives)

## Overview

UNDP describes adaptation in Maldives as a multi-dimensional goal aimed at increasing resilience to climate hazards and supporting sustainable development.

## Core Risk Profile

The page says Maldives is:

- An archipelago of 25 low-lying coral atolls.
- About 1,190 islands, with 358 currently used for settlements, infrastructure, and economic activity.
- Deeply vulnerable to sea-level rise, erosion, and freshwater intrusion.

## Key Numbers

The page states:

- More than 80% of land is less than 1 meter above mean sea level.
- A 1-meter sea-level rise could cause loss of the entire land area.
- 62% of inhabited islands and 45% of tourist resorts reported severe beach erosion.

## Adaptation Priorities

UNDP highlights adaptation actions focused on:

- Coastal zones.
- Infrastructure.
- Tourism.
- Fisheries.
- Water resources.
- Health.
- Agriculture and food security.
- Coral reef biodiversity.

## Policy Context

The page references Maldives' NAPA, the Safer Island Strategy, and climate-resilient development programs and funds.
