# Baa Atoll - UNESCO MAB

## Overview
Baa Atoll Biosphere Reserve is in the central western Maldives in the Indian Ocean. UNESCO describes it as a globally significant marine reserve with a long history of human interaction with the environment and one of the largest coral reef groups in the Indian Ocean.

## Key data
- Year of nomination: 2011
- Country: Maldives
- Total population: 15,005
- Total area: 144,024 ha
- Terrestrial area: 1,040 ha
- Marine area: 142,984 ha
- Location: about 5.11 latitude, 73 longitude

## Ecology
Baa Atoll has a warm, humid tropical climate shaped by two monsoon periods: a rainy monsoon from May to November and a dry monsoon from January to March.

The reserve is mostly marine, with deep ocean around it. Lagoons range from about 30 to 80 m deep, and channels through the atoll can reach 250 to 300 m. The main habitats are coral reefs, islands, seagrass beds, and mangroves.

UNESCO highlights the reefs as the most important habitat for both area and biodiversity. The Maldivian reefs support about 250 coral species, 1,200 reef and reef-associated fish species, marine turtles, manta rays, whale sharks, and seabirds.

## Human geography
Baa Atoll comprises 75 islands, 13 of which are inhabited. The main activities are tourism and fisheries. Resorts are the main economic driver, though tuna and reef fishing remain important, along with handicrafts and tourism supply chains.

## Why it matters
The page frames Baa Atoll as a biosphere reserve where marine biodiversity, tourism, and local livelihoods are tightly linked. It is a useful reference for protected-area status, reef richness, and the role of the atoll in the broader Maldivian conservation story.
