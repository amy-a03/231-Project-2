# Baa Atoll Biosphere Reserve

## Overview
Visit Maldives describes Baa Atoll as a globally significant biosphere reserve in the central Maldives, covering more than 139,000 hectares. The atoll sits just north of the Kaashidhoo channel and contains coral reefs, islands, seagrass beds, and mangroves.

## Biodiversity
The page emphasizes reef biodiversity and the habitat value of the atoll. It notes 1,200 reef-associated marine species, 250 species of hard and soft corals, and populations of marine turtles, manta rays, whale sharks, and seabirds.

It also highlights threatened and endangered species such as green turtles, hawksbill turtles, Napoleon wrasses, and tawny nurse sharks.

## Protection and zoning
Baa Atoll is presented as a strongly managed biosphere reserve with nine core areas where extraction is prohibited. These are surrounded by buffer zones with non-damaging, non-extractive use, and then transitional areas that cover the wider atoll where sustainable activity is permitted.

## Hanifaru Bay
Hanifaru Bay is the standout marine attraction on the page. It is known for large manta ray gatherings, with up to around 100 manta rays feeding in a single congregation when plankton is pushed into the bay by the tide.

## Access
Visit Maldives notes that the atoll is reached through flights from Velana International Airport to Dharavandhoo Airport, with an approximate flight time of 20 minutes.
