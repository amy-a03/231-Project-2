# Eat Like a Local

## Overview
This page focuses on specific regional and island-based dishes, showing how Maldivian food varies across the archipelago while keeping core ingredients and flavors recognizable.

## What it emphasizes
The page highlights dishes such as Addu Bon’di, Naroh Falidha, Golha Riha, Muran’ga Baiy, Haalu Folhi, Masbon’di, and Kashekiyo Foah. It also explains how toddy, Dhiyaa Hakuru, and Karu Hakuru are made and used.

## Dining context
The article encourages visitors staying on inhabited islands to try authentic local food, and it makes clear that local food is readily available in local islands and resorts.

## Why it matters
This is a useful companion source for understanding how "local dining" works beyond the standard resort buffet, especially if you want concrete examples of regional specialties.
