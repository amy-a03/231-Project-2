# Maldivian cuisine: A myriad of rich tastes and flavors

## Overview
This page introduces Maldivian cuisine as a distinct food tradition built around fish and coconut, with influences from neighboring countries and older seafaring contacts.

## Signature foods
It highlights Mas Huni with Roshi as a breakfast staple, Garudhiya and Baiy as a common meal, and side dishes like fried fish, boiled yam, sweet potatoes, and Theluli Faiy. It also mentions Bondibai, Kulhi Boakibaa, Bajiyaa, and Mas Roshi as common snacks or sweets.

## Dining context
The page makes a simple but important point: Maldivian food is readily available in both local islands and resorts, which makes it easy for travelers to compare local and resort dining styles.
