# A Party for Your Palette

## Overview
This Visit Maldives page presents Maldivian food as a blend of island traditions and outside influences, shaped by ingredients such as fish, coconut, spices, starches, grains, and vegetables.

## Dining across accommodation types
The page notes that local resorts, guesthouses, hotels, and liveaboards commonly offer local dishes and drinks in buffets or on request. Maldivian lunches and dinners are often available as special options, alongside vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free choices.

## Drinks and local ingredients
It highlights toddy, sour mango juice, bilimbi juice, seasonal kashikeyo juice, and jasmine-infused black tea as drinks worth trying. It also notes the use of sustainably caught tuna and locally grown produce in many dishes.

## Food examples
The article mentions Mashuni, Garudhiya and Baiy, Masbaiy, Baiypen, Kulhi Baiypen, Kiru boakiba, Bondibai, and Huni Hakuru as examples of dishes and sweets that reflect the range of Maldivian cuisine.

## Why it matters
This is a strong source for how local food shows up across both resort and guesthouse settings, and for the idea that Maldivian cuisine is meant to be tried rather than just observed.
