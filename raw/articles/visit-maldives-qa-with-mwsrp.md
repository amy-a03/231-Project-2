# Q&A with MWSRP

Source page: [Visit Maldives - Q&A with MWSRP](https://visitmaldives.com/en/experience/qa-with-mwsrp)

## Overview

Visit Maldives profiles the Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme as a long-running conservation charity dedicated to studying whale sharks and building community-focused conservation initiatives.

## What the Interview Says

The interview notes that:

- MWSRP started as a scientific expedition in 2006.
- The team has been in the Maldives every year since then.
- There are 647 whale sharks in the database.
- 301 individuals have been re-sighted at least twice.

## Whale Shark Ecology

The article says South Ari Atoll is one of the few whale shark aggregation sites where encounters can happen year-round. It also describes South Ari as having one of the highest re-sighting rates in the world.

## Human Interaction Guidance

The Q&A gives clear encounter advice:

- Do not touch the shark.
- Keep a distance of 3 metres from the body and 4 metres from the tail.
- Do not obstruct the shark.
- Enter the water calmly and from a distance.

## Conservation Role of Citizen Science

The article says guests and operators can contribute by submitting encounter photographs to the Big Fish Network, where photo-identification helps track individuals over time.

## Threats Mentioned

The interview lists threats including:

- Bycatch.
- Targeted fishing and finning.
- Entanglement.
- Vessel collision.
- Habitat destruction and climate change.
- Microplastic.
