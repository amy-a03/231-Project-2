# Where to Stay in The Maldives for All Budgets

Source page: [Wandering Redhead - Where to Stay in The Maldives for All Budgets](https://www.wanderingredhead.com/where-to-stay-in-the-maldives-for-all-budgets/)

## Overview

Wandering Redhead frames the Maldives as more flexible than the classic luxury stereotype suggests. The article compares high-end resorts, mid-range options, and local islands, with practical advice on transfers and budget management.

## How to Get Around

The guide outlines transfer options from Malé:

- Local ferry: cheapest, but slow.
- Speedboat: faster, more expensive.
- Seaplane: fastest and most expensive.
- Domestic flight plus speedboat: another option for farther islands.

## Resort Options

The article mentions several resorts across different budget levels, including:

- Kandolhu
- W Maldives
- Sheraton Maldives Full Moon Resort
- Summer Island
- Paradise Island

It notes that some resorts are still luxury splurges, but others are relatively more affordable or easier to reach from the airport.

## Local Island Options

The guide highlights local-island stays as the best path to a lower-budget Maldives trip, with examples including:

- Maafushi
- Dhiffushi
- Malé

Maafushi is described as a popular base for budget stays and day trips. Dhiffushi is described as easy to reach and more affordable. Malé is presented as a practical overnight stop if flight timing makes a resort transfer awkward.

## Local Island Rules

The article reminds travelers that the Maldives is a Muslim country, so on local islands:

- Dress modestly.
- Avoid public swimwear outside designated beaches.
- Alcohol is not sold on local islands.
- Fridays affect ferry schedules.
