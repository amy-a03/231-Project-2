# Climate Risk Country Profile: Maldives

Source document: [World Bank climate profile PDF](https://climateknowledgeportal.worldbank.org/sites/default/files/2021-05/15649-WB_Maldives%20Country%20Profile-WEB%20%281%29.pdf)

## Overview

The World Bank climate profile frames Maldives as one of the most climate-vulnerable countries in the world because of its low elevation, shoreline dependence, freshwater vulnerability, and exposure to sea-level rise and extreme events.

## Key Climate Risks

The profile highlights:

- Flooding, cyclones, tsunamis, and storm surge.
- Heatwaves and drought.
- Sea-level rise.
- Beach erosion.
- Saltwater intrusion into freshwater lenses.
- Threats to coral reefs and fisheries.

## Maldivian Exposure

The report says:

- More than 80% of land is less than 1 meter above sea level.
- A 1-meter sea-level rise could cause loss of the entire land area.
- 62% of inhabited islands and 45% of tourist resorts have reported severe beach erosion.
- Coral reef ecosystems have exceptional biodiversity and critical protective and economic value.

## Sea-Level and Temperature Projections

The profile includes projections showing rising temperatures, uncertain but potentially increasing precipitation, and major sea-level-rise scenarios. It notes that sea level rise could exceed 0.5 m by 2100 under higher emissions scenarios.

## Adaptation Context

The page also describes adaptation planning, including the Maldives NAPA, the Safer Island Strategy, and the need for climate-resilient infrastructure, coastal-zone risk reduction, and water-security measures.
