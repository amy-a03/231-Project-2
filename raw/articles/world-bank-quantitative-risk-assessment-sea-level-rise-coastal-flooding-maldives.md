# Quantitative Risk Assessment of Sea Level Rise-Induced Coastal Flooding in Maldives

Source document: [World Bank PDF](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099120224122594569/pdf/P180529-56857528-fa06-4fd0-846e-bfe33eaa66cf.pdf)

## Overview

This World Bank background paper quantifies how sea-level-rise-induced coastal flooding affects Maldives assets and islands. The report supports the Maldives Country Climate and Development Report.

## Main Findings

The report says:

- Sea level rise is a key threat because Maldives is low-lying and economically concentrated near the shoreline.
- About 72% of land is at 1.5 m above mean sea level or less.
- Roughly 36% of buildings are within 100 m of the shore.
- About 71% of buildings are within 200 m of the shore.
- The total estimated capital stock modeled is around US$24.5 billion.

## Exposure and Damage

The report models:

- Coastal flooding at 1-in-10, 1-in-100, and 1-in-1000-year return periods.
- Flood depth classes of 0.2 m, 0.5 m, and 1 m.
- Asset exposure and physical damage across islands and atolls.

It notes that airports are relatively protected because they are highly elevated, while buildings clustered near coasts are more exposed.

## Planning Implication

The paper concludes that relatively mild adaptation can bring large economic benefits, but more detailed island-level flood protection information is needed to refine risk estimates.
