# Roadmap for Fostering Coral Reef Resilience in the Maldives

Source document: [World Bank PDF](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099120224122513519/pdf/P180529-b06465d6-6ff3-49a8-bb19-3f4cb30217e3.pdf)

## Overview

This World Bank background paper examines how climate change affects fisheries, coastal ecosystems, and coastal communities in Maldives, with a strong focus on adaptation pathways.

## Main Findings

The paper says climate change will:

- Intensify marine heatwaves in Maldives.
- Drive large increases in degree-heating days for coral areas.
- Reduce fish catches in the EEZ under multiple climate scenarios.
- Shift habitat suitability and species turnover across the archipelago.

## Fisheries and Coastal Communities

The report states that fisheries are extremely important to Maldives because they support food, livelihoods, and local economies, especially where tourism is absent.

It recommends:

- Conserving marine ecosystems.
- Increasing socioeconomic resilience.
- Exploring alternatives to fisheries.
- Expanding research and capacity building.

## Reef and Nature-Based Adaptation

The paper recommends:

- Marine spatial mapping.
- Coral reef restoration.
- Mangrove and reef nature-based solutions.
- Better reef-fish monitoring.
- Community participation in habitat monitoring.
