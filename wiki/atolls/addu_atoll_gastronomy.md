# Addu Atoll Gastronomy

## Summary
Addu Atoll in southern Maldives offers a distinctive food culture blending South Indian, Arabian, and Sri Lankan influences. The region provides authentic local dining experiences that contrast with typical resort cuisine, making it an ideal destination for exploring traditional southern Maldivian food culture.

## Key Facts
- Addu cuisine blends South Indian, Arabian, and Sri Lankan culinary influences
- Signature Addu dishes include Addu Reha Curry, Theleli Folhi, Addu Havaadhu, and Addu Bondi
- Coconut-based curries are central to local food preparation
- Traditional dining includes eating by hand and pairing meals with tea
- King coconuts from local Gaadiya vendors are a notable local food product
- Addu offers distinctive regional cuisine less influenced by resort dining culture
