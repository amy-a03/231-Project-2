# Atoll Diving Techniques

## Summary
Atoll diving in the Maldives emphasizes drift diving along current-swept walls and channels, with exploration of thilas, caverns, and cleaning stations. The technique is suited to relaxed pacing and suits both intermediate and experienced divers.

## Key Facts
- Drift diving is the primary technique, propelled by monsoon currents that sweep along island chains and atoll walls
- Thilas are reef pinnacles rising from lagoon floors, offering varied topography and concentrated marine life
- Swim-throughs, caverns, and overhangs provide technical features within atoll structures
- Cleaning stations are specific sites where wrasse and shrimp service manta rays and other megafauna
- Current strength is significant; diving suits experienced planners and divers comfortable with relaxed, current-assisted pacing
