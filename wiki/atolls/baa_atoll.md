# Baa Atoll

## Summary
Baa Atoll is a major tourism region in the Maldives listed as a key booking destination on Booking.com's directory, indicating substantial resort availability and traveler interest.

## Key Facts
- Listed as a major atoll region in Maldives tourism directories
- Significant booking concentration on Booking.com
- One of the primary atoll destinations for tourists
- Baa Atoll is UNESCO-recognized
- Featured as a premier diving destination
- Contains coral reef systems suitable for diving
- Located at approximately 5.11°N latitude, 73°E longitude in the central western Indian Ocean
- Total area of 144,024 hectares: 1,040 hectares terrestrial, 142,984 hectares marine
- Comprises 75 islands, 13 of which are inhabited
- Lagoon depths range from 30–80 meters; atoll channels reach 250–300 meters depth
- Supports approximately 250 coral species and 1,200 reef and reef-associated fish species
- Primary economic activities: tourism (resorts), tuna and reef fishing, handicrafts
- Designated as UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 2011
