# Baa Atoll Biosphere Reserve

## Summary
Baa Atoll is a UNESCO-designated biosphere reserve in central Maldives covering over 139,000 hectares, protecting diverse coral reef ecosystems, seagrass beds, and mangroves. The atoll is renowned for its exceptional marine biodiversity and is managed through a three-zone protection system balancing conservation with sustainable tourism.

## Key Facts
- Located north of the Kaashidhoo channel in central Maldives
- Covers more than 139,000 hectares of marine and terrestrial habitat
- Home to 1,200 reef-associated marine species and 250 species of hard and soft corals
- Contains nine core protection zones where extraction is prohibited, surrounded by buffer zones and transitional areas
- Protects threatened species including green turtles, hawksbill turtles, Napoleon wrasses, and tawny nurse sharks
- Accessible via 20-minute flight from Velana International Airport to Dharavandhoo Airport
- Populations include marine turtles, manta rays, whale sharks, and seabirds
