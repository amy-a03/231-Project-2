# Coastal Flooding and Sea-Level Rise in Atolls

## Summary
Scientific research on wave-induced flooding in atoll islands, with specific application to the Maldives. The study develops a model-based method to estimate flood risk that varies significantly by island morphology and wave climate, enabling prioritized adaptation planning.

## Key Facts
- The Maldives has 1,192 coral reef islands, with most having land elevations below 1 meter above present-day mean sea level
- Flood risk depends strongly on island morphology and wave climate, requiring island-by-island assessment
- The study analyzed 56 Maldivian islands with available elevation and reef-length data
- Wave-induced flooding can be rapidly assessed under different sea-level rise scenarios
- The method helps determine required elevation increases and prioritize intervention by island
- Adaptation strategies should be ranked by individual island exposure and vulnerability
