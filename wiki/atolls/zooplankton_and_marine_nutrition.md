# Zooplankton and Marine Nutrition

## Summary
Zooplankton form the base of tropical marine food webs, supporting filter-feeding megafauna like manta rays and whale sharks. Seasonal oceanographic conditions concentrate these microscopic organisms, creating predictable feeding pulses that large marine animals depend on for survival.

## Key Facts
- Zooplankton are concentrated by bay geometry, currents, tides, and plankton movements
- Southwest monsoon creates nutrient-rich feeding opportunities in Hanifaru Bay
- Warming oceans may reduce tropical zooplankton biomass, threatening dependent species
- Plankton sampling uses specialized nets towed by freedivers in protected areas
- Zooplankton pulses support both manta rays and whale sharks in the Maldives
