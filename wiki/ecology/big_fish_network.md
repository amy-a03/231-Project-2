# Big Fish Network

## Summary
A citizen-science platform created by the Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme that enables tourism operators and guides to document whale shark encounters and build stewardship. The network generates high-quality data on whale shark sightings while fostering responsibility among tourism stakeholders.

## Key Facts
- Allows guides and operators to submit whale shark sightings and upload encounter photos
- Provides access to identification archives for individual shark tracking and life history documentation
- Offers training workshops for guides to ensure consistent data collection quality
- Includes online portal and mobile app for members and guests to report sightings
- Features leaderboards and report posters to incentivize operator participation and engagement
- Builds communication networks and responsibility among tourism operators
- Generates consistent, high-quality data for whale shark research and conservation
- Guests and operators can submit encounter photographs
- Uses photo-identification to track individual whale sharks over time
- Supports MWSRP research database and monitoring efforts
- Enables distributed data collection across multiple atolls
