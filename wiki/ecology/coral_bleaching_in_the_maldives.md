# Coral Bleaching in the Maldives

## Summary
Coral bleaching is a major threat to reef health in the Maldives, monitored through comprehensive multi-institutional research combining citizen science and expert datasets. The IUCN tracks bleaching as part of broader reef management and resilience efforts.

## Key Facts
- The 2016 Maldives coral bleaching report is the most comprehensive monitoring effort to date in the region
- Data collection combines citizen-science and expert-scientist datasets from government, academic, and NGO sources
- Coral bleaching is treated as a major reef-management topic rather than an isolated event
- Bleaching research is integrated with resilience, restoration, and management resources in IUCN taxonomy
- Multi-institution collaboration on reef monitoring is essential for tracking bleaching status
- Current status: Bleaching Warning alert level
- Recent change: Alert upgraded to Bleaching Warning (positive indicator of increased stress)
- Virtual station provides live signals of bleaching pressure across the country
- Data relevant for reef managers and tourism stakeholders
- Part of comprehensive 5km resolution satellite monitoring system
- Repeated thermal anomalies documented in Maldivian waters
- Bleaching events directly correlate with coral cover decline
- Recovery trajectories vary by location and reef type
- Bleaching impacts cascade through fish and macroinvertebrate communities
