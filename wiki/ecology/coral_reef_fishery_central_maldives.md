# Coral Reef Fishery - Central Maldives

## Summary
A baseline study examining reef fishery conditions in Central Maldives and the impact of tourism development on fish catch demand. The research found sustainable yield levels in 2006-2007 but recommends continued monitoring as tourism demand increases.

## Key Facts
- Tourism development increased demand for fresh fish from local fishermen to supply resorts directly
- 2006-2007 catch composition survey showed no alarming sustainability trends
- Fish yields remained below published thresholds for unsustainable fisheries
- Study serves as important baseline for reef-fish management in atoll ecosystems
- Monitoring of fishery status needed as tourism demand continues to grow
