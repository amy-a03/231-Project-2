# Coral Reef Watch Monitoring

## Summary
Coral Reef Watch is an operational satellite-based monitoring system developed by NOAA that tracks heat stress on coral reefs globally using sea-surface-temperature data. It provides real-time assessment of bleaching conditions across ocean basins.

## Key Facts
- NOAA NESDIS operates Coral Reef Watch as satellite-based monitoring system
- Uses sea-surface-temperature data as primary indicator of heat stress
- Historical data collection extends from 1985 to present
- Covers Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Ocean basins
- Identifies bleaching-level conditions and heat stress alerts
- Serves as operational tool for tracking global coral bleaching events
