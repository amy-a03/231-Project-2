# Coral Resilience in Maldivian Reefs

## Summary
A 19-year longitudinal study examining how coral reefs in the Maldives respond to climate change and human pressure. The research demonstrates that reef resilience is highly site-specific, with minimal human impact correlating to better recovery rates.

## Key Facts
- Study spanned 19 years across four Maldivian reef sites
- Reefs with minimal human impact showed superior recovery capacity
- Lagoon sites and heavily visited reefs exhibited more variable recovery patterns
- Rasdhoo Madivaru oceanic site maintained stable coral cover throughout study period
- Recovery patterns differed significantly across sites and time periods
- Study used Reef Check surveys measuring benthic composition, fish abundance, and macro-invertebrate abundance
