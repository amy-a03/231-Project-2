# Coral Restoration in the Maldives

## Summary
Large-scale coral restoration efforts pioneered by Four Seasons resorts following the devastating 1998 bleaching event, now a central component of marine conservation strategy.

## Key Facts
- Four Seasons launched one of the Maldives' earliest large-scale coral restoration programs after 1998 bleaching event
- More than 500,000 coral fragments planted since 2001
- Coral frame building conducted at Marine Discovery Centres
- Resorts promote reef-safe sunscreen use among guests and staff
- Conservation work embedded into daily resort operations and guest education
- Six Senses Laamu can grow 10,000 baby corals annually
- 2.86 million coral larvae released onto house reef as of 2024
- Coral restoration supports reef ecosystem recovery and resilience
- Part of broader marine conservation strategy in the Maldives
- Combines scientific research with on-site breeding and restoration techniques
