# Global Coral Bleaching 2014-2017

## Summary
The 2014-2017 coral bleaching event was the third documented global bleaching event and the longest, most widespread, and possibly most damaging on record. More than 70% of the world's coral reefs experienced bleaching-level heat stress during this period, driven primarily by a strong El Niño in 2015-2016.

## Key Facts
- Third documented global bleaching event in recorded history
- Longest, most widespread, and possibly most damaging bleaching event on record
- More than 70% of world's coral reefs experienced bleaching-level heat stress
- Strong El Niño in 2015-2016 was primary driver of prolonged heat stress
- Alert Level 2 heat stress corresponds to widespread bleaching and significant mortality
- Impacted multiple ocean basins: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian Ocean, Southeast Asia, Australia, and Caribbean
