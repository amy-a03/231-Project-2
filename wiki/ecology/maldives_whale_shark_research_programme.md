# Maldives Whale Shark Research Programme

## Summary
A conservation charity and research organization focused on studying whale sharks in the Maldives and Indian Ocean. The programme combines scientific research, community engagement, and citizen science to support whale shark conservation and inform policy.

## Key Facts
- Focuses on whale shark population dynamics and movement patterns in the Maldives
- Combines research, community mobilization, and citizen science approaches
- Works to inform conservation policy and management decisions
- Involves tourism operators and local communities in conservation efforts
- The Maldives is one of the world's best-known whale shark destinations
- Maintains documentation and databases of individual whale sharks for tracking
- 185 contributors have participated in data collection
- 726 individual whale sharks documented with over 8,000 encounters recorded
- Population shows 72.5% male sex bias
- Research tracks movements, size, sex ratios, threats, and environmental influences (lunar cycles, tides)
- Research provided baseline data for creation of South Ari Marine Protected Area, Maldives' largest protected area
- Programme promotes open access and free information distribution to benefit visitors and local operators
- Founded in 2006 as a scientific expedition
- Research team has been present in the Maldives every year since 2006
- Database contains 647 identified whale sharks
- 301 individuals have been re-sighted at least twice
- Operates citizen science programme through Big Fish Network for photo-identification
