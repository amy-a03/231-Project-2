# Manta Rays in the Maldives

## Summary
The Maldives hosts the world's largest recorded reef manta ray population, with seasonal migrations driven by monsoons and zooplankton distribution. Visitors can encounter these rays responsibly by following a code of conduct and contributing to conservation efforts.

## Key Facts
- The Maldives has the largest recorded reef manta ray population in the world based on 15 years of Manta Trust data
- Reef manta rays are the common species, with seasonal visits from oceanic manta rays
- Manta rays migrate across the archipelago according to monsoon shifts and zooplankton availability
- Hanifaru Bay in Baa Atoll is a major feeding aggregation site
- Sustainable encounter practices include calm water entry, respectful distance, side approach, no touching, and respecting cleaning stations
- Visitors can contribute to conservation by submitting encounter photographs to Manta Trust ID databases
