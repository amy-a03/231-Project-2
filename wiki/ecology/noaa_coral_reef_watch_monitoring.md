# NOAA Coral Reef Watch Monitoring

## Summary
NOAA Coral Reef Watch provides satellite-based monitoring of coral reef heat stress and bleaching conditions globally. The program uses daily 5km satellite data and four-month heat-stress outlooks to track and forecast bleaching events.

## Key Facts
- Operates daily 5km resolution satellite bleaching alert system
- Provides four-month heat-stress outlook forecasts
- Uses Alert Level 1 (significant bleaching) and Alert Level 2 (widespread bleaching and mortality) classification system
- Monitors coral reef heat stress across all major ocean basins
- Tracks prolonged heat stress accumulation and regional impacts
- 5km near-real-time bleaching monitoring network covering global coral reef regions
- Combines satellite data to surface current stress levels, time-series graphs, and data files
- Provides email alert system for reef managers and visitors
- Uses expanded bleaching alert levels (some stations still transitioning from older system)
- Data accessible through regional virtual station list and downloadable files
