# Reef Manta Ray Feeding Behavior

## Summary
Reef manta rays in Hanifaru Bay demonstrate selective feeding behavior triggered by specific environmental conditions. Research shows they switch to active feeding when zooplankton biomass exceeds critical density thresholds determined by tidal cycles.

## Key Facts
- Manta rays exhibit threshold-based feeding behavior with prey density switching point at 53.7 mg dry mass per cubic meter
- Feeding events are strongly associated with high zooplankton biomass
- Tidal cycles drive zooplankton patch formation and manta foraging aggregations
- Manta rays can detect and respond to predictable prey density conditions
- Understanding feeding ecology supports conservation and sustainable tourism management
