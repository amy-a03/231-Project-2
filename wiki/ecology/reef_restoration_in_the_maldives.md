# Reef Restoration in the Maldives

## Summary
A conservation approach using coral fragment transplantation and electric reefs to help damaged reefs recover and build resilience. The Maldives has pioneered electric reef technology since the 1990s.

## Key Facts
- The first electric reef in the Maldives was built in 1996 at Dhawa Ihuru
- A second electric reef was created in 1998
- Banyan Tree Vabbinfaru built the third electric reef at its house reef in 2001
- Coral fragments are cultivated in rope nurseries and transplanted to restore damaged reefs
- In 2016, 1,000 coral fragments were transplanted onto each coral rope nursery at Banyan Tree Vabbinfaru
