# Sea Level Rise and Coastal Flooding in Maldives

## Summary
The Maldives faces severe vulnerability to sea level rise and coastal flooding due to its low-lying geography and concentrated coastal development. A World Bank quantitative risk assessment models flood impacts across islands and atolls, with findings showing that 72% of land is at or below 1.5 m above mean sea level.

## Key Facts
- Approximately 72% of land in Maldives is at 1.5 m above mean sea level or less
- About 36% of buildings are located within 100 m of the shore
- Approximately 71% of buildings are within 200 m of the shore
- Total estimated capital stock modeled at around US$24.5 billion
- Coastal flooding assessed at 1-in-10, 1-in-100, and 1-in-1000-year return periods
- Flood depth classes modeled at 0.2 m, 0.5 m, and 1 m increments
- Airports are relatively protected due to elevated construction, while coastal buildings face higher exposure
