# Thermal Stress and Reef Health

## Summary
Thermal stress is the primary driver of coral bleaching in the Maldives, tracked through satellite-based heat monitoring systems. Understanding current thermal conditions is essential for predicting bleaching events and planning conservation responses.

## Key Facts
- Measured through 5km resolution satellite data
- Real-time monitoring enables early warning of bleaching conditions
- Bleaching risk directly correlates with water temperature anomalies
- Alert levels help managers respond proactively to reef stress
- Accessible through NOAA virtual station system with downloadable data
