# Whale Shark Conservation

## Summary
Conservation efforts focused on understanding and protecting whale shark populations in the Maldives. Research-driven approach emphasizes population dynamics, threat assessment, and habitat protection through marine protected areas.

## Key Facts
- Whale shark population in Maldives shows male-biased sex ratio (72.5% male)
- Conservation informed by tracking movements, size variations, and environmental influences
- Threats to whale sharks documented and studied
- Open access to research information supports community-based conservation
- Marine protected areas provide critical habitat preservation
- Whale sharks visit during rainy season when plankton abundance increases
- September and October offer the best chances to see whale sharks
- Whale shark presence correlates with plankton-rich waters
- Primary threats include bycatch, targeted fishing, finning, entanglement, and vessel collision
- Habitat destruction and climate change pose significant conservation challenges
- Microplastic pollution affects whale shark populations
- Citizen science through guest photo submissions supports long-term monitoring
- South Ari Atoll is a critical aggregation site with year-round encounters
