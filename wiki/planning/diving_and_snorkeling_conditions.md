# Diving and Snorkeling Conditions

## Summary
Water visibility for diving and snorkeling varies significantly by season, with peak conditions during the dry months and reduced clarity during plankton blooms in the wet season. Understanding seasonal patterns helps optimize underwater exploration.

## Key Facts
- Best visibility period: January through April, especially February and March
- Visibility reduced May through September due to increased plankton in the water
- Favorable diving conditions support advance booking during peak months
- Plankton blooms during wet season attract filter-feeding marine megafauna
- October and November combine improved visibility with whale shark and manta ray presence
- January provides excellent diving conditions with recommended dives at Banana Reef
- February is the driest month with ideal water conditions for snorkeling and underwater activities
- March allows easy snorkeling and diving using the resort's house reef
- May water visibility is lower, making it not ideal for diving due to rainfall and higher humidity
- June through October visibility is reduced due to plankton and monsoon conditions
- Northern atolls experience fewer rainy days in July compared to southern atolls
