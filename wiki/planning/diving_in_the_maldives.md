# Diving in the Maldives

## Summary
The Maldives is a year-round dive destination featuring warm waters (80–86°F), drift diving along nutrient-rich currents, and abundant marine life including manta rays, whale sharks, and giant turtles. Diving styles range from atoll exploration and thila dives to caverns and cleaning stations.

## Key Facts
- Water temperatures remain warm year-round at 80–86°F (27–30°C), allowing diving in any season
- August–November offers peak encounters with manta rays and whale sharks; January–April provides best visibility and driest weather
- Drift diving dominates due to monsoon currents that sweep nutrients along walls and channels of the archipelago
- Major dive areas include South Ari Atoll, North Ari Atoll, Baa Atoll, North Malé Atoll, South Malé Atoll, Vaavu Atoll, Noonu Atoll, Dhaalu Atoll, Raa Atoll, Fuvahmulah, and Addu Atoll
- Common topographic features include thilas (reef pinnacles), swim-throughs, caverns, overhangs, and cleaning stations where manta rays gather
- Velana International Airport in North Malé Atoll serves as the primary international gateway; secondary airports include Hanimaadhoo and Gan
- Liveaboard diving is a primary way to experience multiple sites across the atoll system
- Currency is MVR (Maldivian Rufiyaa); time zone UTC+05:00; voltage 230V; country code +960
- January through April is recommended for best diving conditions and underwater visibility
- Dive sites include coral reefs, shipwrecks, and advanced areas such as Fuvahmulah Atoll
- Baa Atoll is UNESCO-recognized for diving
- Many dive sites are distributed across the broad area of nearly 1,200 islands
