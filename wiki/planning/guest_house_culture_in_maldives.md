# Guest House Culture in Maldives

## Summary
Guest houses represent a community-based accommodation model that emerged after tourism regulations were liberalized, allowing visitors to stay on inhabited islands and experience everyday Maldivian life. They offer an alternative to resort tourism, emphasizing local dining, cultural interaction, and authentic island experiences.

## Key Facts
- Guest houses expanded following changes to Maldivian tourism regulations that permitted accommodation on inhabited islands
- They provide access to local food culture including tea shops and hedhikaa (traditional Maldivian snacks)
- Guest house stays enable interaction with local Maldivian communities rather than isolated resort experiences
- Part of a broader accommodation ecosystem alongside resorts, hotels, and liveaboard vessels
- Offer a more resident-like experience compared to traditional resort tourism
