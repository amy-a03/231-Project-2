# Hanifaru Bay

## Summary
Hanifaru Bay is a key feeding hotspot in the Maldives where reef manta rays aggregate to forage on tidally driven zooplankton patches. The bay is an important marine protected area with significant conservation and economic value for sustainable tourism.

## Key Facts
- Located in the Maldives as a major reef manta ray feeding site
- Features tidally driven, high-density zooplankton patches that attract manta aggregations
- Manta rays feed when zooplankton density reaches a threshold of 53.7 mg dry mass per cubic meter
- Designated as a marine protected area with economic importance for tourism
- Large manta aggregations occur predictably based on tidal and environmental conditions
- Largest known aggregation site for reef manta rays with over 250 individuals identified during peak feeding events
- Located in the Maldives
- Peak feeding occurs during the southwest monsoon season
- Bay geometry, currents, and tides trap and concentrate zooplankton
- Protected bay restricts standard plankton sampling methods
- Located within Baa Atoll Biosphere Reserve
- Known for gatherings of up to approximately 100 manta rays in a single congregation
- Manta rays aggregate during plankton blooms when tide pushes food sources into the bay
- Recognized as a standout marine wildlife destination in the Maldives
