# Liveaboard Diving

## Summary
A liveaboard is a dive vessel where guests eat, sleep, and dive from the same platform, typically lasting 7 to 14 nights with four or five dives per day. Liveaboards are especially useful for accessing remote dive sites and are popular in destinations like the Maldives.

## Key Facts
- Trips typically last 7 to 14 nights with four or five dives per day
- Selection depends on experience level, comfort/amenities, and dive focus
- Some itineraries focus on strong currents and deep dives; others emphasize comfort, big animals, or macro photography
- Reputable liveaboards maintain emergency oxygen, first aid equipment, and detailed dive planning
- Divers must respect depth limits, buddy procedures, and surface intervals
- The Maldives is described as a strong liveaboard destination due to the scattered atoll system and distance between sites
- Liveaboards provide access to multiple dive areas (South Ari, North Ari, Baa, Vaavu, etc.) in a single trip
- PADI offers guides to liveaboard operators and booking resources as primary planning tools
- Liveaboards suit divers seeking relaxed drift diving pacing and extended exposure to atoll ecosystems
- Liveaboards include lodging, food, and diving in one package
- Allow flexibility to follow fish and current conditions across multiple sites
- Provide access to multiple dive locations without being anchored to a single resort
- Advanced certification often required for intense itineraries
