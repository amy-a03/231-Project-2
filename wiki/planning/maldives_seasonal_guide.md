# Maldives Seasonal Guide

## Summary
The Maldives experiences distinct seasonal patterns that affect weather, marine life activity, and tourism pricing. The best time to visit depends on individual priorities: dry season offers reliability for beach lovers, while shoulder and low seasons provide better value and unique conditions for divers and surfers.

## Key Facts
- High season (December–April) is dry, sunny, and calm with peak prices and advance booking requirements
- Shoulder season (May–August) offers better value, active marine life (manta rays, whale sharks), and strong surfing conditions
- Low season (September–November) is the wettest period with short heavy downpours but dramatic skies and the lowest resort rates
- June and July offer the lowest accommodation prices for budget travelers
- May through October provides the best surfing swells
- Dry season (December–April) provides calmest water conditions ideal for divers
