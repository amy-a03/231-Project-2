# Monsoon Seasons

## Summary
The Maldives experiences two distinct monsoon periods: the Southwest monsoon (May–August) and Northeast monsoon (September–December). Both bring occasional rain and stronger winds, but showers are generally brief and do not significantly disrupt travel plans.

## Key Facts
- Southwest monsoon: early May to late August
- Northeast monsoon: late September through December
- Monsoon periods bring occasional rain and stronger winds
- Showers during monsoon seasons are typically brief with quick sunshine returns
- Monsoon season offers lower prices and quieter resorts for budget travelers
- Monsoon currents sweep along the archipelago year-round, carrying nutrients that support marine life and drive drift diving
- August–November monsoon period is peak season for manta rays and whale sharks due to nutrient influx
- January–April offers the best visibility, driest weather, and warmest daytime temperatures
- Water temperatures remain consistent at 80–86°F throughout the year, enabling year-round diving
- Current strength varies with monsoon intensity, requiring divers to adjust pacing and planning accordingly
- Dry season runs December through April with temperatures around 30-32°C and excellent underwater visibility
- Rainy season extends May through November with peak rainfall in September and October
- Showers during rainy season are typically heavy but short-lived, with warm sunny conditions still common
- Rainy season offers lower flight and resort prices with fewer tourists
- September and October are wettest months but attract surfers and divers seeking manta rays and whale sharks
- Rainy monsoon season: May to November
- Dry monsoon season: January to March
- Creates warm, humid tropical climate in the Maldives
- Shapes lagoon conditions and marine ecosystem dynamics in Baa Atoll
