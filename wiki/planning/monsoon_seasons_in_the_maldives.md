# Monsoon Seasons in the Maldives

## Summary
The Maldives has two distinct seasons: a dry season from January to April with minimal rainfall and excellent visibility, and a wet season from May to December characterized by increased rain, storms, and higher humidity. Despite wetter conditions, the wet season offers lower prices and attracts marine wildlife.

## Key Facts
- Dry season runs January through April with average rainfall of 2-5 inches per month
- Wet season spans May through December with rainfall ranging from 6-9 inches per month
- Average daytime temperatures remain consistent year-round between 84°F and 90°F
- Average overnight lows rarely fall below 75°F throughout the year
- Even during wet months, long bright sunny spells are common
- Humidity is notably lower during the dry season (January-April)
- High season runs December through April during the dry northeast monsoon with lower rainfall and humidity
- Southwest monsoon established by June brings cloudy days, frequent rain, and possible heavy downpours
- February is the driest month of the year with average temperature around 82°F
- October is the wettest month, though southwest monsoon begins to ease by month's end
- Water visibility is lower during monsoon season (May-October), making it less ideal for diving but good for marine life spotting
- Humidity is notably lower during February-March compared to monsoon months
- Dry season runs November to April with relatively low rainfall, especially January through March
- Wet season runs May to October with some islands receiving up to nine inches of rain per month
- Temperatures remain warm year-round, typically in the 80s Fahrenheit
- Humidity exists throughout the year regardless of season
- Short afternoon storms are common during wet season but consecutive sunny days still occur
