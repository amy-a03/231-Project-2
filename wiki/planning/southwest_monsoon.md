# Southwest Monsoon

## Summary
The southwest monsoon season brings strong winds and ocean currents to the Maldives that concentrate nutrient-rich zooplankton. This seasonal phenomenon creates peak feeding conditions for manta rays and whale sharks at locations like Hanifaru Bay.

## Key Facts
- Southwest monsoon concentrates zooplankton through currents and water movement
- Peak manta ray feeding at Hanifaru Bay occurs during southwest monsoon season
- Creates nutrient-rich feeding pulses for filter-feeding megafauna
- Oceanographic conditions trap plankton through bay geometry and tidal patterns
