# Zooplankton Dynamics

## Summary
Zooplankton distribution and density in Hanifaru Bay are driven by tidal cycles, creating predictable high-density patches. These patches are essential food resources that trigger reef manta ray aggregations.

## Key Facts
- Zooplankton biomass is measured in mg dry mass per cubic meter
- Tidal cycles create predictable, tidally-driven zooplankton patches
- High-density zooplankton patches attract large manta ray aggregations
- Zooplankton density threshold of 53.7 mg/m³ triggers manta feeding behavior
- Understanding zooplankton dynamics is key to predicting manta presence and behavior
